Pamela Jo Lee

Graduate Researcher • Columbia, SC

I am a second-year Marine Science Master’s student at the University of South Carolina in Dr. Subra Bulusu’s Satellite Oceanography Lab, where I study freshwater, mesoscale, and submesoscale dynamics in the Gulf of Mexico. I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences from UCLA (2023). My work combines satellite remote sensing, altimetry, and river records to quantify sea-surface-salinity variability and the influence of river discharge and the Loop Current.

Interests & Research

My research focuses on satellite-observed surface salinity and its coupling with coastal river discharge and the Loop Current System in the Gulf of Mexico. I quantify how river discharge and Loop Current evolution together control basin-wide sea-surface-salinity (SSS) patterns during flood and drought years (Lee et al., 2025). DOI:10.1029/2024GL114409
